3. The answer form

The answer form contains a block for your name (A), a block for your matriculation number (B) and a block for the answers (C). Fill in the boxes for your name, signature and matriculation number first. Note that the machine will not try to interpret your handwriting after the examination. These boxes are only for documentation purposes.

Mark the boxes for your matriculation number carefully as the computer will need these marks to identify your results. Mistakes in the block with your matriculation number can lead to delays and misinterpretation.

How does the computer assign the answer form to the right person?

a)       The system reads your name using optical character recognition software.
b)       Your signature is scanned using software used for finger prints in criminal investigations.
c)       Only the crosses in the boxes under your matriculation number are interpreted.
d)       The system reads your matriculation number using optical character recognition software.
